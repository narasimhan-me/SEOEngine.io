# EngineO.ai Brand Guide

This document defines the core branding elements for EngineO.ai.

## Brand Name

**EngineO.ai**

- Full name: EngineO.ai
- Short handle: EngineO
- Descriptor: Discovery Engine Optimization (DEO) Platform

### What is DEO?

DEO (Discovery Engine Optimization) = SEO + AEO + PEO + VEO

- **SEO** – Search Engine Optimization (classic web search results)
- **AEO** – Answer Engine Optimization (AI assistants, answer boxes, chat search)
- **PEO** – Product Engine Optimization (on-site search, marketplaces, product feeds)
- **VEO** – Video Engine Optimization (YouTube, Shorts, TikTok, and other video surfaces)

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**EngineO.ai** is a **Discovery Engine Optimization (DEO) platform** for modern brands, ecommerce, SaaS, and content-driven sites.

**Core positioning:**

- **Primary statement:** "Optimize any website for search & AI discovery."
- Treats search engines and AI assistants as a single discovery surface.
- Sits above your existing stack instead of replacing ecommerce platforms, CMSs, or custom sites.

**Supported platforms (high level):**

- Ecommerce stores (e.g., Shopify and other modern platforms).
- CMS and marketing sites (blogs, docs, content hubs, landing pages).
- Headless and custom web apps built on frameworks like Next.js.

Keywords:

- Automated
- Intelligent
- Reliable
- Growth-focused
- Modern & clean

---

## 2. Brand Voice

Tone:

- Confident but not arrogant.
- Helpful and practical.
- Clear, non-jargony, but can be technical when needed.
- Results-driven, focusing on outcomes (traffic, revenue, time saved).

Writing guidelines:

- Prefer short sentences.
- Use active voice.
- Avoid fear-based language.
- Use examples when explaining technical concepts.
- Emphasize benefits over features (“Save 10+ hours a week” vs “Has automation”).

---

## 3. Tagline

Primary tagline:

> **"Optimize any website for search & AI discovery."**

Alternatives:

- "Discovery Engine Optimization for search + AI."
- "One DEO platform for products, content, and entities."
- "Make your brand discoverable everywhere."

---

## 4. Logo Guidelines

### 4.1 Concept

Recommended concept: **Engine + Growth**

- Combination of a subtle gear/engine motif with an upward graph or arrow.
- Minimal, flat design (no skeuomorphic bevels or gradients).

### 4.2 Usage

- Default logo: icon + wordmark (`EngineO.ai`).
- Simplified icon-only version for:
  - Favicon
  - App icons
  - Social avatars

Clear space:

- Maintain at least `0.5x` of the icon size as padding around the logo.

Do not:

- Distort or stretch.
- Recolor arbitrarily.
- Place on low-contrast backgrounds.

---

## 5. Colors

Primary palette:

- **Primary Blue:** `#2563EB`
  - Used for CTAs, links, key highlights.
- **Dark Slate:** `#111827`
  - Main text, nav bars.
- **Soft Gray:** `#6B7280`
  - Secondary text.
- **Background Light:** `#F9FAFB`
  - Page backgrounds.
- **Accent Green:** `#16A34A`
  - Success states, positive indicators.

Usage examples:

- Buttons: blue background, white text.
- Backgrounds: mostly light with dark text.
- Charts and metrics: mix of blue and green.

---

## 6. Typography

Primary font: **Inter**

- Headings: Inter, weight 600–700.
- Body: Inter, weight 400–500.

Fallbacks: system fonts (e.g. `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif`).

Hierarchy:

- H1: 32–40px
- H2: 24–28px
- H3: 20–22px
- Body: 14–16px
- Captions: 12–13px

---

## 7. Imagery & Illustration

Style:

- Clean, minimal dashboard screenshots.
- Line-art or flat, vector-style illustrations.
- Avoid stock photos of “people in suits shaking hands”.

Themes:

- Dashboards & analytics.
- Automation and AI.
- E‑commerce storefronts.

---

## 8. Component Design Principles

Buttons:

- Rounded corners (6–8px).
- Primary: solid blue.
- Secondary: white with blue border.
- Hover states must be visually distinct.

Forms:

- Simple, uncluttered.
- Clear error messages.
- Labels outside the inputs.

Cards:

- White background.
- Subtle shadow.
- Rounded corners (8–12px).
- Clear header and content area.
